# PaisaWatch: Source Brief for a Podcast (NotebookLM Audio Overview)

Use this document as the source you upload to NotebookLM. It is written to be turned
into a warm, curious, slightly provocative but always factual two-host conversation
for a general Indian audience. Every number below is real and comes from official
public data surfaced on paisawatch.live. Nothing here is invented.

---

## The one-line version
PaisaWatch is a free, non-partisan website that shows an Indian citizen two things
they have paid for their whole life but have never actually seen: where their tax
money goes, and what the people who spend it declared about themselves. Every figure
links back to its official source. paisawatch.live

---

## The big idea: two numbers you were never shown
There are two kinds of numbers in a person's financial life. The ones they are shown,
and the ones they are not. The gap between them is where most of their money quietly
lives.

- The first hidden number is personal. You get a raise. It feels like progress. But a
  raise is a nominal number, and inflation is the tax nobody sends you a bill for. If
  your salary went up 9 percent and prices went up 6 percent, you did not get 9 percent
  richer. Your real gain is closer to 3 percent, and that is before income tax takes a
  slice of the raise itself. Some years the raise and inflation cancel out and you have
  run hard just to stay in the same place. Most people never run that number.

- The second hidden number is public. Every month, before your salary even reaches your
  account, tax leaves. Over a working life it is a staggering sum. It becomes roads,
  schools, hospitals, defence. It is the single largest purchase most people ever make,
  and they make it involuntarily, in installments, for decades. And they have never been
  handed a receipt. You know exactly what your phone bill bought. You have no idea what
  your tax bought.

PaisaWatch was built to hand a person both numbers.

---

## What is actually inside the tool
1. A map of public money. It tracks MPLADS public-works spending across India by state
   and by sector, using official data.gov.in records from the Ministry of Statistics and
   Programme Implementation.
2. A lookup for your own representative. Type your constituency and see what your MP or
   MLA declared about themselves: assets, pending court cases, education, and, for MPs,
   parliamentary attendance and activity. Every figure links to the original affidavit or
   record.
3. Everyday-money calculators. Income tax estimator, EMI, SIP, real returns versus
   inflation, and a lifetime tax contribution estimate. No login, no ads.

## Real numbers to ground the conversation
These are figures currently shown on the site. Present them as "declared" and "on the
tool right now," not as final verdicts.

Public spending, at a glance:
- Rs 10,839 crore of MPLADS public-works spending tracked.
- 485 Lok Sabha MPs covered.
- 3,725 MLAs across 30 sitting assemblies.
- 3,552 representatives with declared assets of a crore or more.
- 1,892 representatives who declared at least one pending criminal case.
- 30 states and union territories covered.

Where that MPLADS money went, by sector:
- Roads, rail and bridges: Rs 4,501 crore
- Public facilities: Rs 2,644 crore
- Education: Rs 1,538 crore
- Others: Rs 1,079 crore
- Drinking water: Rs 729 crore
- Sanitation and health: Rs 348 crore

Where the national rupee comes from (Union Budget 2024-25):
- Borrowings and other liabilities: 27 percent
- Income tax: 19 percent
- GST and other taxes: 18 percent
- Corporation tax: 17 percent
- Non-tax receipts: 9 percent
- Union excise duties: 5 percent
- Customs: 4 percent

A striking talking point: borrowing is the single largest source of the national rupee,
larger than income tax. The country spends now and repays later, with interest.

## The rules that make this trustworthy (hosts should stress these)
This is the most important part. It is what separates PaisaWatch from a rage-bait page.

- The villain is never a party or the government. The villain is opacity, the habit of
  being kept in the dark. That framing is deliberate, and it is why both sides of the
  political aisle can share the tool without either calling it biased.
- Pending is not proven. A pending case is a declared, unresolved legal matter. It is not
  a conviction, and showing it is not an allegation of guilt. The tool links the original
  record so you judge, not the tool.
- Declared is not audited. Affidavit figures are self-declared by candidates. PaisaWatch
  presents them as declared, not independently verified.
- Absence of data is data too. Where a verifiable public record cannot be found, the tool
  says so and shows a coverage count rather than inventing a number. For example, a count
  like "485 of 543" means the rest have no published affidavit, not that they are clean
  and not that they are guilty.
- Size is not a scandal. A large state spends large sums. A big number is not wrongdoing.
  The question is what the money bought and whether it was tendered fairly.
- It is free, independent and non-partisan. No login, no ads, no side.

## Why it matters
When records are hard to find, the incentive to cut corners grows, and the cost of
checking falls on the ordinary citizen who has neither the time nor the tools. A
democracy that runs on rumour instead of records is a fragile one. The fix is boring and
powerful: a single, official, machine-readable, regularly updated public register of who
represents you, what they declared, and how public funds were used, downloadable by
anyone, free, forever. PaisaWatch is a citizen-built step toward that, made from the
records that already exist but were never made easy to read.

## How a listener can use it in two minutes
1. Open paisawatch.live and look up your own constituency. Read what your MP or MLA
   declared, and open the source.
2. Go to the calculators and enter your salary from a few years ago and today. See the
   real change after inflation.
3. Then decide for yourself. The tool does not tell you what to think. It hands you the
   numbers you already paid for.

## Natural conversation beats (to make the audio feel alive)
- Open on the gut-punch: can you actually name your own MP, and do you know where a single
  rupee of your tax went?
- The salary reveal: the moment a host realises their last raise may not have beaten
  inflation.
- The "wait, I can check this myself?" moment when they look up a real representative.
- The careful, honest turn: reminding listeners that pending is not guilt, and that the
  point is the source link, not the accusation.
- The hopeful close: this is not anti-anyone, it is pro-daylight. End with a clear nudge
  to visit paisawatch.live and look up their own seat.
